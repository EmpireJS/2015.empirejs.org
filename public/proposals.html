Our Call for Talks and Workshops
is now closed!

We'd love for everyone and anyone who is doing amazing things with Node.js to submit a proposal to EmpireNode 2014. The conference will take place in New York, NY on October 27th, 2014. If you're coming from out of town we should be able to cover all of your travel (unless you live in space, in which case you're awesome and good luck!).

The deadline to submit proposals is September 2, 2014 11:59pm EDT.

Diversity of voices is important to our community, and we are serious when we say EVERYONE AND ANYONE. If you've never given a talk before that's EVEN MORE reason to submit a proposal here now. If you have any questions about what we're looking for, or you're just needing a little encouragement for your submission, please drop us an email at speakers@empirenode.org

Remember, when submitting your proposal that being respectful and kind matters to us. If you're curious about what we mean, please read more about our thoughts on this in our Code of Conduct below.

Our Conference Format

EmpireNode is a single day, single track format with a twist.

Talks

The morning will be action-packed 20-25 minute talks covering the range of all things Node.js. Presenters are encouraged to be as creative as possible with their proposal topics, talks, and format. Looking for suggestions of topics? How about:

  • Real-time and distributed systems
  • Modules, modularity, and npm
  • Did we mention streams? No? Then streams!
  • Databases with LevelDB and friends
  • Web protocols (DNS, SMTP, etc)
  • Real-world Node.js in Production
  • Robots, copters, boats and other hardware!
  • V8 Optimizations
  • Inside Node.js core

Just remember to make it shine!

Workshops

Where it gets interesting is the afternoon fun times centered around a number of workshops, each presented by a team with lead instructor, delivering content for 90-120 minutes. We look forward to workshops that are interactive and well-documented.

The workshops will wrap up with talks given by the lead instructor on what was missed while attendees were enjoying the other workshops. The focus here is to share a summary of the session and demonstrate the more advanced excitement you could have with this material. We encourage team proposals for the workshop submissions and are happy to help you find volunteers to help teach the material. We do ask that only one presenter talks at the wrap-up session for each workshop.

NYC = No Yutzes Central

By attending EmpireNode you agree not to be a yutz. Harassment of any kind will not be tolerated. We'd like to thank our friends at JSConf for the longer, less yiddish filled Code of Conduct we happily uphold.
